### F2 — Vendor `public_status:` field is now diverging in shape across vendors — schema iteration warranted

After ingest 8, the four vendor `public_status:` fields look like:

- **circle**: `["NYDFS-Money-Transmitter (Circle Internet Financial, LLC, since 2015-09)", "NYDFS-VCBA / BitLicense (Circle Internet Financial, LLC, since 2015-09)", "BMA-Digital-Asset-Business (Circle International Bermuda Limited)"]`
- **sky-protocol**: `[]` (empty — deliberately, no licenses surfaced)
- **bitgo**: `["NYDFS-Limited-Purpose-Trust-Charter (BitGo New York Trust Company, LLC, since 2021-03)", "OCC-National-Trust-Bank-Charter (BitGo Bank & Trust, National Association)"]`
- **chainalysis**: `[]` (empty — SaaS provider, not a regulated digital-asset entity)

The flat-list-of-strings structure is starting to embed multiple semantic axes per entry: license type, legal entity name, jurisdiction, issue date. Combined with the meaningful difference between "vendor has no licenses surfaced" (Sky) and "vendor is structurally not a regulated entity" (Chainalysis), the field is doing more work than its shape supports.

**Two schema-iteration options:**

**Option A — Rich `licenses:` structured field replacing `public_status:`**:
```yaml
licenses:
  - type: "Money Transmitter"
    issuer: "NYDFS"
    legal_entity: "Circle Internet Financial, LLC"
    issue_date: "2015-09"
    source: "[[regulator-nydfs-bitlicense-page-2026-04]]"
```

Advantages: structured, queryable (could populate sector-level tables programmatically), explicitly source-attributed per license.
Disadvantages: more verbose, breaks compatibility with any future tooling expecting flat string lists.

**Option B — Add `regulated_entity: true|false|saas-provider` and keep `public_status:` flat**:
```yaml
regulated_entity: true   # or false (DeFi-native) or saas-provider (Chainalysis)
public_status: ["NYDFS-Money-Transmitter (Circle Internet Financial, LLC, since 2015-09)", ...]
```

Advantages: minimal change; explicitly captures the structural difference between "no licenses surfaced" and "not a regulated entity."
Disadvantages: doesn't address the within-string-format ambiguity.

**Verdict**: defer to a future schema-iteration commit. The 4-vendor sample is small enough that a single canonical answer isn't yet load-bearing. **When a 5th or 6th vendor enters the wiki and applies similar pressure, decide between Option A and Option B.** I'd lean Option A (richer structured field) for query-side benefits in a deployed wiki, but Option B is the lower-friction path.

**Action**: no schema iteration in this lint commit. Tracked for the post-Session-2 first-real-iteration session.
