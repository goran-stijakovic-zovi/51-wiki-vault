# Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) — ESMA Page Content

## Overview and Scope

The Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) institutes uniform EU market rules for crypto-assets. According to ESMA:

> "The regulation covers crypto-assets that are not currently regulated by existing financial services legislation."

Key regulatory objectives include:

> "Key provisions for those issuing and trading crypto-assets (including asset-reference tokens and e-money tokens) cover transparency, disclosure, authorisation and supervision of transactions."

The framework aims to support market integrity and financial stability by:

> "regulating public offers of crypto-assets and by ensuring consumers are better informed about their associated risks."

## Covered Asset Categories

MiCA addresses three primary categories:

1. **Asset-Referenced Tokens (ART)** — covered under Title III
2. **E-Money Tokens (EMT)** — covered under Title IV
3. **Other Crypto-Assets** — covered under Title II (excluding ART and EMT)
4. **Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs)** — covered under Title V

## White Paper and Disclosure Requirements

Proper disclosures take the form of a white paper. ESMA states:

> "Proper disclosures are essential for safeguarding investors by allowing them to make informed decisions about a given crypto-asset."

White paper formatting entered into application on **23 December 2025**, requiring **iXBRL format** to ensure information is machine-readable and consistent with technical standards.

**Critical Notice from ESMA:**

> "The crypto-asset white papers listed in ESMA's register have not been reviewed or approved by any competent authority in any Member State of the European Union."

## Implementation Timeline

- **Entry into Force:** June 2023
- **Full Application:** December 2024
- **Transitional Period:** 18 months (ending 1 July 2026)
- **White Paper iXBRL Requirement:** 23 December 2025 (application date)
- **MiCA Order Book/Record Keeping JSON Schema:** Published 28 November 2025; NCAs expect compliance within 6 months

## Transitional Provisions (Article 143)

Member States may implement two transitional measures:

1. **Grandfathering Clause (Art. 143(3)):** Entities providing crypto-asset services per national law before 30 December 2024 may continue until 1 July 2026 or until authorisation decision.

2. **Simplified Authorisation (Art. 143(6)):** For entities authorised under national law on 30 December 2024.

## Registration and Authorisation

ESMA maintains an interim MiCA register containing:

- White papers for crypto-assets other than ART and EMT
- Issuers of ART
- Issuers of EMT
- Authorised crypto-asset service providers
- Non-compliant entities

The interim register operates via **weekly updates** as CSV files until mid-2026, when formal integration into ESMA's IT systems occurs.

## Member State Notification Requirements

Member States must notify ESMA and EBA of:

- Designated competent authorities (Article 93)
- Laws implementing Title VII (Article 99) — deadline: 30 June 2025
- Administrative penalties rules (Article 111) — deadline: 30 June 2025
- Complaints-handling procedures (Article 108)
